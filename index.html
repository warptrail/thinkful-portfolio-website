About Me

Developing

The World Wide Web is the frontier of our age. Some call it the Information Superhighway. I seek to contribute towards its continued growth in such a way that enhances people’s lives. Making the web better will save people precious time to do more living.

The way we code it today will affect the world of tomorrow. The web is still relatively young. New generations who are raised on it will surely find ways to solve problems we don’t even know we have yet. I ask myself one important question when beginning a project:

"How will this add value to somebody's life?"

Networking

The web is called the web because it is the mesh that brings computers and their users together. Anybody on the planet can potentially say hello to anybody else. It’s rather amazing.

I do not intend to code in a vacuum. I hope my creations can be shared and integrated with your creations. I seek to learn from others and stand on the shoulders of giants.

Recharging

Although it is easy for one to live life online all the time, I'm a big fan of disconnecting on a regular basis to recharge. I have many artistic endeavors and am an eternal student of philosophy and science. Yoga is essential to life as a developer, so I can recover from the screen time. My side gig is searching for the Lost City of Atlantis.
